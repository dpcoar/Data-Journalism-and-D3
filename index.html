Correlations Discovered Between Health Care and Poverty Levels

After analyzing the 2014 state-level data comparing poverty rate to uninsured percentages, I found a positive correlation between the two. The data shows that those states with the highest poverty rates also have the highest percentages without health care. In addition, some of the worst cases, Texas, Georgia and Mississippi have refused Medicaid expansion putting many lives at risk.
